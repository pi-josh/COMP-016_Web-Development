All Souls' Day
November 2

All Souls' Day, observed on November 2, is a day dedicated to remembering and honoring the souls of the departed. It is traditionally a time for families to visit the graves of their loved ones, offering prayers, lighting candles, and placing flowers on tombstones. The day serves as a solemn reminder of mortality and the importance of remembering those who have passed away.

Many cultures celebrate All Souls' Day with special customs, including preparing altars with photos and mementos of deceased relatives. In some traditions, families hold gatherings to share stories and memories of their loved ones, fostering a sense of connection even after death. The observance of All Souls' Day encourages reflection on life, loss, and the enduring bonds of love that transcend time.
