Valentine's Day
February 14

Valentine's Day, celebrated on February 14, is a day dedicated to love and affection between intimate partners. It originated as a Christian feast day honoring Saint Valentine, but has since evolved into a broader celebration of romantic love. On this day, people express their love for one another through gifts, cards, and special gestures.

Common traditions include giving flowers, chocolates, and heartfelt notes or love letters. Many couples go out for romantic dinners, while some choose to spend a cozy evening at home. Valentine's Day serves as a reminder to cherish and appreciate loved ones, celebrating the bonds that bring joy and happiness to our lives.
